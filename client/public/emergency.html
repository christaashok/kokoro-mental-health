Immediate Coping Strategies

  • Breathing Exercise: Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, breathe out for 6. Repeat 5 times.
  • Grounding Technique: Name 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste.
  • Call Someone: Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or counselor.
  • Change Your Environment: Go to a safe, comfortable space or step outside for fresh air.
  • Use Ice: Hold ice cubes or splash cold water on your face to redirect intense emotions.
  • Write or Journal: Express your feelings on paper to help process them.

Create Your Safety Plan

A safety plan is a personalized guide that helps you stay safe during a crisis. Consider including:

  • Warning signs that a crisis may be developing
  • Coping strategies that have worked for you
  • Trusted people you can call for support
  • Professional contacts (therapist, doctor, crisis line)
  • Safe environments and people who help you feel better
  • Steps to make your environment safe (remove means of harm)

Remember: You are not alone. Your life has value. Help is available.
